# Skill: Backlink swaps

Backlinks are the hardest part of SEO to get right. Free mode helps you find relevant sites to approach. The paid tier is a real, vetted exchange network that does the matching for you.

## Free mode (no key, no cost)

You can find candidates with web search. You cannot arrange a real, safe exchange by yourself, and you should be honest about that.

### 1. Find relevant, non-competing sites

For the site's niche, search for sites that publish to the same audience without selling the same thing:

- `<niche> blog`, `<niche> resources`, `best <niche> blogs`
- `<niche> "write for us"`, `<niche> "guest post"`, `<niche> "contribute"`
- Roundups and resource pages: `<niche> "useful resources"`, `<topic> tools list`

### 2. Qualify each candidate

For each, note: what they publish, whether they are a direct competitor (skip those), whether they look active (recent posts), and a contact or submission path if one is visible.

### 3. Hand back a shortlist

Return 10 to 20 qualified candidates with the URL, why they fit, and the contact path. Suggest a specific page on the user's site that would be a natural, genuinely useful link target for each.

### What free mode will not do

- It will not fabricate domain authority, traffic, or backlink counts. Those need paid data.
- It will not pretend a cold list is a "network". Real link exchange needs trust on both sides, which is what the paid tier provides.
